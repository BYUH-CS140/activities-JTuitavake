Ha'amonga 'a Maui

The Stone Trilithon




Maka faakinanga stone throne

The Ha'amonga 'a Maui is a monumental stone trilithon located in Niutōua, on the eastern side of Tongatapu, Tonga. Constructed in the 13th century during the reign of King Tuʻitātui, it is a significant symbol of Tonga's ancient heritage and engineering prowess.

The structure consists of three massive coral limestone slabs, with two upright pillars supporting a horizontal lintel, each weighing several tons. Historically, it is believed to have served as a royal gateway or throne for the King, symbolizing strength and unity among the people. Legend ties its construction to Maui, a mythical Polynesian hero, further enhancing its cultural and spiritual significance. Today, the Ha'amonga 'a Maui stands as an enduring testament to Tonga’s rich history and a site of national pride and wonder.
